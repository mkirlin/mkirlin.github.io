I am originally from West Des Moines, IA, a suburb of Des Moines, IA. I went to grade school and high school there, and then I attended Creighton University in Omaha, NE for my undergrad and various post-graduate activities.

What I Did, For A While:

In undergrad I majored in Spanish and International Relations. I'm mostly fluent in the former, and somewhat fluent in the latter. The plan after that was law school, also at Creighton. This turned out to be a terrible plan.

After a semester of law school, I realized law wasn't for me. So I left law school. I decided to get an MA in education, a path I had contemplated before settling on the safer choice of law school.

(If you're at all familiar with the legal job market at the moment, you're probably laughing already. Go ahead and get it out, I'll wait.)

I finished my MA, but I realized that I wasn't content with teaching. I had the opportunity to work in a classroom equipped with iPads, and I realized a few things while I worked there.

  • Technology, utilized effectively, can dramatically improve the classroom experience, BUT
  • Technology, as it exists today, is near-useless in the average classroom

What I wanted to do was build the tools that teachers and parents could use to actually use technology in a way that complemented and enhanced the classroom experience the way that I know it can. I looked around for what skills I would need to build those tools, and I found Dev Bootcamp.

(Whoa. Ok, so that was kind of heavy.)
