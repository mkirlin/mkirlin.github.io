Week 5 Cultural Blog

10 June 2014

What was it like to pair with others to solve challenges?

It's been kind of challenging. Parts of it have been a lot of fun; I had a session last week that was really rewarding, and at the end of it I really felt like I had learned a lot. However, there have also been pairings where I've felt superflouous, pairings where I've felt frustrated because I've outstripped the other person, and pairings where we just didn't click. Overall, I think I'm liking it slightly more than I'm disliking it, but it is definitely raising conflicting feelings in me.

Was something particularly fun or rewarding?

The pair chellange last week - challenge 6, debugging the North Korean missile code - was a ton of fun. At the end of it, I felt like I had a really firm grasp of a lot of the subjects we were working on and I had a great time working with my pair.

Was something frustrating and difficult for you?

I have had two pairing sessions where I felt like we weren't really on the same page, and the pairing suffered from that. I also think there's a side of my personality that has very little patience or sympathy for people who don't get things quickly, and I have had a pairing session or two where I was short with my partner because I was feeling somewhat contemptuous of the other person. That's a problem with my attitude, though.

How did you feel when you read your feedback?

Mostly pretty good, with a side helping of unhappiness. There were a few pieces of feedback that were positive with qualifications, and I tend to read that as criticism. But I also recognize that I have a tendency towards being overbearing - it's a familial trait - and that came through in some of the feedback.

Was the feedback you received helpful to your learning?

It was. I know some of the things I need to work on; I've spent a long time reprogramming my own social interactions, but I know I have a lot of ground left to cover. It's always good to get feedback on those things. I feel like you'll never improve if you don't get good feedback, and I need that interaction.

Based on the feedback you've received, what are you going to do to improve next time you pair?

I am constantly trying to relinquish the lead, and not be too "RAH RAH LEADERSHIP." That's the hardest thing for me, and I'm going to keep working on not dominating interactions.

How was it to write feedback?

Surprisingly difficult. I tried to keep ASK in mind when I was writing feedback and it was difficult. Being anything other than critical was difficult. I am conditioned to give feedback in a different way, and it has proved difficult to give good feedback in DBC.

What was most difficult for you when writing feedback for your pair?

It has been different aspects of it. Sometimes, I don't have anything I can think of that's actionable, other times it's tough to be specific. Sometimes you just want to say "Be better!", but that really isn't an option, and I know that, but the lizard brain doesn't really know that.
