Pick one value and write a couple of sentences about why one of the values you chose is important to you.

Confidence is incredibly important to me because I believe that to some degree everything else flows from confidence. If you are not confident, it is difficult to do everything. If you believe that you can do something, or even if you're just capable of projecting the appearance of believing in yourself, you can achieve amazing things. I have noticed a consistent pattern in my life and the lives of others that when people are relatively equal in ability, but one is more confident than the other, they tend to be more successful.

I first started noticing this is one skill in my own life: speaking another language. When I carefully think through what I'm saying, translate every word with caution, insist on conjugating every verb perfectly, and generally overthink the process, I couldn't speak the language. But when I am able to set that overly-perfectionist mind aside and just talk, trusting in my abilities, I could carry on conversations with native speakers, and even if I made errors, they just didn't matter that much. When I began to pay attention to it, I started to realize that the effect carried through elsewhere in life: if you approach a situation with confidence in yourself, the experience will go more smoothly. It is especially effective in interpersonal relationships. If you are able to project confidence, even if you're just faking it convincingly, other people will be drawn to you. Confidence is the fountain from which everything else flows, and so it is the most important.
